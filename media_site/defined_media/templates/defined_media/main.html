ACRONYM is a SQL database that can be browsed

ACRONYM is a database aimed at bringing together genomic information and organism growth data. As the wealth of sequence information continues to grow, there is an expanding pool of publically-avaiable genomic data. A possible application for these data is to elucidate defined media to culture the so-called "uncultured majority" of organisms that have not yet been isolated in a laboratory setting. ACRONYM brings together fully-sequenced organisms and their associated chemically-defined media as reported in primary literature sources. Our hope is that this set of experimentally-verified data will establish a knowledgebase of media-genome connections that will expand and aid in media formulation development.

Media formulations and genomic data also feed into the field of constraint-based metabolic modeling, in which the genome is related to metabolism through gene-protein-reaction relationships and used to create a model that simulates growth on a particular media formulation. ACRONYM was formed with a strong emphasis on constraint-based modeling and contains data for the majority of organisms with existing in silico models (Palsson reference). The information in ACRONYM provides a wealth of media conditions that can contribute to model creation and refinement efforts.

ACRONYM was created by the Price lab at the Institute for Systems Biology in Seattle, WA and the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

How To Use ACRONYM

For the most part, ACRONYM is very simple to use. You can browse the data via the website, or download the entire data here.
